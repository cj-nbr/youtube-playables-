# Number Slide — Complete Guide

Number Slide is a modern, relaxing take on the classic 15-puzzle. You are faced
with a 4×4 grid filled with numbered tiles from 1 through 15 and a single empty
space. Your task is to slide the tiles until they read 1 to 15 in order, left to
right and top to bottom, with the blank sitting in the bottom-right corner. There
is no timer and no way to lose — the only real challenge is solving the board in
as few moves as you can.

## What is Number Slide

The 15-puzzle was first popularized in the 1880s and became a global craze. The
idea is beautifully simple: fifteen numbered tiles and one gap on a 4×4 board.
Because only one tile can move at a time, the puzzle feels fair and tactile.
Number Slide keeps that spirit while removing pressure, making it a calm,
thinking-person's pastime rather than a speed race.

Every board you are given is generated to be solvable. The tiles are scrambled by
actually sliding the blank around, which guarantees that a solution exists. You
will never be handed one of the impossible configurations that half of all random
arrangements produce, so you can always finish what you start.

## How sliding works

Sliding is simple but strictly constrained. Only a tile that sits directly next
to the empty space can move, and it swaps places with the blank. There is no
dragging a piece across the board; every move is a single orthogonal step into the
gap. Diagonal moves are not allowed, and that restriction is exactly what gives
the puzzle its structure and challenge.

On desktop you can click a tile next to the blank, or use the arrow keys to slide
the neighboring tile in the pressed direction. On mobile, a single tap on an
adjacent tile performs the move. Because each move is reversible, you can always
step a tile back if you change your mind.

## The row-by-row method

The most reliable way to solve Number Slide is the row-by-row method. Start with
the top row: place tile 1 in the top-left corner, then slide tiles 2, 3, and 4
into place across the top. Once the top row is locked, move to the second row,
then the third, and finally arrange the bottom row with the blank in the corner.

When you reach the last two rows, the board gets tighter. Solve tiles 13 and 14 in
the third row first, then position the remaining pieces so that 15 and the blank
land correctly. Working systematically, rather than chasing random slides, is the
fastest route to a clean solve.

## Managing the empty space

Managing the empty space is the real skill. Keep the blank in the region you are
still solving, ideally near the bottom-right, so you never accidentally slide a
finished tile out of place. When you need to nudge one tile without undoing
others, rotate three tiles around the blank in a small cycle — this lets you
reposition a single piece precisely.

Protect solved rows at all costs. Once the top rows are correct, route the blank
around them rather than through them. If you must pass the blank over a finished
tile, restore that tile immediately before moving on.

## Keeping your solve efficient

Efficiency comes from planning. Number Slide is untimed, so count your moves and
try to beat your previous best. A clean solve means fewer slides, so think one
move ahead and backtrack deliberately when stuck instead of scrambling the board.
With the row-by-row method, careful blank management, and a little patience, even
a heavily scrambled grid becomes a satisfying puzzle you can solve with confidence.
