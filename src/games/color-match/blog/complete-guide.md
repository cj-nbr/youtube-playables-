# Color Match — Complete Guide

Color Match is a quick, addictive puzzle that hides real color science behind a
simple premise: look at a target color, rebuild it with three sliders, and submit
before the timer runs out. Below is everything you need to understand the game and
start climbing the leaderboard.

## How Color Match works

Each round shows a target swatch at the top of the screen and gives you a mixing
area with three sliders labeled R, G, and B. Every channel runs from 0 to 255. As
you drag the sliders, your "your mix" swatch updates live alongside a hex readout,
so you always know exactly what color you have built. You get 30 seconds per round
and there are 10 rounds in a full session.

The controls are deliberately minimal. There is a **Submit** button to lock in your
guess and a **Skip** button to abandon a round you cannot solve. When the timer hits
zero, your current mix is scored automatically, so you are never stuck — but acting
deliberately is almost always better than letting the clock decide for you.

## Understanding the scoring system

Scoring has two parts. The first is accuracy, measured by the Euclidean distance
between your mixed RGB values and the target's RGB values. In plain terms, the game
computes how far apart the two colors are across all three channels and converts
that gap into points: a tiny gap means a high score, a large gap means a low one.
A perfect, exact match is worth the maximum accuracy reward, which tops out near
1000 points for a single round.

The second part is a time bonus. Because the timer is short, the game rewards
players who submit quickly. The earlier you commit an accurate color, the larger
the speed bonus stacked on top of your accuracy score. This is why the best players
are not only precise but fast — hesitation quietly costs points.

## Mastering the time bonus

The time bonus decays as the clock ticks, so there is a real trade-off between
polishing your mix and submitting early. A useful rule of thumb: once you are within
a few units on every channel, submit. Chasing the final single digit often costs
more in lost bonus than it gains in accuracy. Learn to recognize when "close enough"
is mathematically the optimal move.

## Core strategies for high scores

Start with the dominant channel — the value that is highest or furthest from the
others — and rough it in before touching the smaller channels. Then use the hex
readout to match digits channel by channel rather than trusting your eyes alone,
which can be fooled by similar hues. Finally, watch the timer and commit the moment
you are accurate. Small, deliberate slider nudges beat large sweeping guesses every
time.

## Common mistakes to avoid

New players tend to over-correct with big slider jumps, ignore the timer until it
is too late, and waste seconds chasing perfection on an especially tricky color.
When a target feels unreachable, Skip to protect your average. Combine precise
adjustments, early submissions, and smart skips, and your cumulative score will
rise steadily toward the top.
