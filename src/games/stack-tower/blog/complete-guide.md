# The Complete Stack Tower Guide

Stack Tower is a calm but deceptively tense one-tap timing game. A single block slides left and right across the top of the screen, and with one click or tap you drop it onto the tower below. The block locks into place, any part that overhangs the edge is sliced away, and the next block takes the exact width of the overlap. That simple loop is the whole game, yet it hides a surprising amount of strategy.

## How the core mechanic works

The heart of Stack Tower is overlap. Because each new block is only as wide as the portion that landed on the block beneath it, every drop permanently changes the shape of your tower. A perfectly centered drop keeps the width identical, which is the ideal outcome. An off-center drop trims the overhang and shrinks the next block, making future placements tighter and riskier.

This is what makes the game feel fair. You are never penalized by random events; the state of your tower is entirely the product of your timing. A careless early drop does not just cost you one block, it quietly reduces the margin for error on every block that follows.

## Scoring and endless play

There is no final level and no win screen. Your score is simply the number of blocks you manage to place, so the only real goal is to survive as long as possible and beat your own high score. The challenge ramps up because the block speed increases with every level, giving you less and less time to judge each drop.

That endless design is part of the appeal. A run can last ten seconds or ten minutes depending on your focus, and the leaderboard is always your previous best. Chasing a new personal record is what keeps players coming back for one more tower.

## Building good habits

The most important habit is to wait for the block to reach the center of the stack before dropping. Centered drops preserve width, and a wide tower is a forgiving tower. Pair that with a steady rhythm; tapping on the beat of the block's movement is far more reliable than reacting at the last possible moment.

Protect your width early. When the tower is wide, small mistakes are recoverable. Once it narrows, every drop matters, so play safer and accept a good overlap rather than gambling on a perfect one.

## Common mistakes to avoid

The biggest mistake is chasing perfection on a fast-moving block and missing entirely. A small overlap keeps you alive, while a complete miss ends the run at zero overlap. When in doubt, drop a touch early where the wider base below can still catch the block.

Another subtle error is tapping faster as the speed rises. The input never needs to be fast; it needs to be timed. Stay calm, read the swing, and let consistency carry your score upward. Master those habits and your towers will climb higher than you expect.
